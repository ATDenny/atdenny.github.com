Experience

Pilot CE750 | Baker Aviation

  • August 2020 ‐ Present

Returning to the flight line, I am currently employed as a pilot crewmember for Baker Aviation, flying the Cessna Citation X CE750. Based out of Ft. By returning to On-Demand Charter operations, I've been able to renew my operational commitment to customer service. As a customer-facing member of Baker Aviation, it is the goal to continously exceed customer expectations during all interactions.

Program Manager | FlightSafety International

  • February 2018 ‐ July 2020

Until July of 2020, I acted as a Program Manager for the Dassault Falcon 2000 and Falcon 900EX programs for the DFW-N Learning Center. My duties included overseeing all daily operations of both programs, such as: instructor/sim qualifications, & scheduling, client records, progress & satisifaction. I was responsible for the successful installation and integration of the VITAL 1100 visual display on the DA2000 LVL D sim, and the Honeywell Primus 2000 Elite II avionics suite with SVS for the DA900EX LVL D sim. I was also an instructor and TCE for the Pilatus PC-12/47 and PC-12/47E programs, conducting checks and tests for a variety of NAAs. I conducted FAR135.293/.297 checks as a CCA for 45+ individual FAR135 Operators.

This position made me continue to grow as a manager, responsible for 6-10 direct-report instructors and programs worth millions of dollars on an annual basis. It also allowed me to hone my technical skills and knowledge bases as a pilot and advanced pilot instructor.

First Officer | Red Wing Aeroplane

  • January 2016 ‐ January 2018

As First Officer on Cessna Citation 500 Series aircraft, based out of Red Wing, MN, I assisted Captains in all phases of flight. This included pre-flight and performance planning duties, utilizing ARINCDirect software. I flew ~1000 hrs in 2 years, gaining invaluable operating experience in all types of flight conditions in Citation II, V, and Ultras. Regular operations into high-density traffic airports, remote airfields with minimal services, and mountainous terrain airports all gave me a strong operational foundation - which I still rely on to this day.

My time with Red Wing Aeroplane set me on a path where FAR135/91K operations have taken center stage in my career. The operational variety, combined with the need to be thoughtful, independent and resourceful drew me to this sector of the industry.

Class Manager | Transpac Aviation Academy

  • March 2014 ‐ December 2015

As a Class Manager and Part 141 Stage Check Instructor for Transpac, based out of Deer Valley, AZ I oversaw a team of 12-20 CFIs (direct reports) and 65-90 Ab-Initio cadets from a variety of central-, eastern-, and southeastern-Asian Air Carriers. In addition to providing primary instruction and conducting Stage Checks, my duties included crew pairing and scheduling, instructor indoc, standardization, qualification and record keeping. Additional duties included cadet indoc, qualification, mediation, and support.

This position was my first to include managerial duties in addition to instructional duties. I gained critical experience in areas such as team member mediation, data analysis, scheduling projections and liaising with high-value accounts.

CFI | US Aviation Academy

  • November 2013 ‐ February 2014

After a year in Grand Forks, I returned home and accepted a position as an instructor with US Aviation Academy in Denton, TX. After working primarily with collegiate students training at a slower pace at UND, US Aviation Academy gave me an introduction into accelerated Part 141 Ab-Initio style training. I worked primarily with Air China cadets, and it was at US Aviation Academy where I had my first student solo sign-off as a CFI. Ultimately I would leave after 4 months due to an opportunity to progress my career with Transpac Aviation Academy.

This brief introduction into sharing a flightdeck with someone with whom I did not share a native language was where I began to be more effective at implementing CRM strategies to ensure the safe operation of the aircraft at all times.

CFI | University of North Dakota

  • October 2012 ‐ October 2013

After completing my flight training in March‐August of 2012, I was hired by UND's Aerospace department as a CFI. I am thankful to have been given the opportunity to join such a prestigious institution as the University of North Dakota. UND's reputation is well-earned. The faculty and facilities are second to none, and I credit my year in Grand Forks with instilling me with core values that have guided my career ever since - an unerring commitment to safety and customer service. My time at UND was mainly spent as a CFII, focusing on Instrument and Comm SEL collegiate courses.

UND is where I made the transition from a private pilot mindset to a professional pilot mindset. Even now years later, I still routinely draw upon lessons learned there on how to prepare for any flight.
